# Governance

Umami Compass uses a maintainer-led, open-development model.

## Decisions

- Bug fixes and small improvements are decided through review based on correctness, security, compatibility, maintenance cost, and user value.
- New public tools, transports, authentication modes, or management capabilities begin with an issue and documented threat/compatibility analysis.
- Significant architectural decisions are recorded as ADRs in `docs/adr`.
- The repository owner has final responsibility for releases, security response, package ownership, and resolving deadlock.

## Maintainers

Maintainers are expected to review respectfully, disclose conflicts of interest, protect embargoed vulnerability information, and use the documented release process. New maintainers may be invited after sustained, trustworthy contributions across code, review, documentation, or community support.

## Changes to governance

Governance changes are proposed publicly and should include motivation, transition impact, and the responsibilities of any new role.
